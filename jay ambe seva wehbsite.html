Story about Bhadarvi Poonam

The Bhadarvi Poonam fair at Ambaji is a large religious gathering where millions of devotees walk to the temple town to pay homage to the goddess Amba during the Bhadarvi (Bhadarva) month's full moon (Poonam). The fair lasts for several days, with pilgrims arriving on foot from various parts of Gujarat and beyond. The event is organized by the Ambaji temple trust and the district administration, who provide facilities like sanitation, drinking water, food, and security for the large influx of devotees. Jay Ambe Seva Kendra USA also organizes a special seva camp during this time, giving service of food, medical support, and resting arrangements to serve the walking devotees with devotion and care.

It is believed that prayers offered during Bhadarvi Poonam are heard directly by the Goddess, and devotees receive her boundless blessings. In this spirit of devotion, Jay Ambe Seva Kendra serves pilgrims with heartfelt seva, helping them on their sacred journey to seek Maa Amba’s divine grace.

Our Story and Mission

Jay Ambe Seva Kendra USA was founded with the sole purpose of fostering spiritual growth and community service, inspired by the divine grace of Shree Ambe Maa. For 25 years, we have dedicated ourselves to organizing auspicious events and camps, providing a platform for devotees to connect with their faith and contribute to selfless service (seva).

**As part of our commitment to seva, we proudly offer comprehensive medical help, nourishing meals (breakfast, morning tea, lunch, evening tea, and dinner), comfortable resting arrangements, and vibrant Garba experiences with Gujarat's finest singers, ensuring every pilgrim's well-being and spiritual joy.**

Our annual camp in Danta is a testament to our commitment, bringing together hundreds of pilgrims to partake in sacred rituals, spiritual discourses, and communal activities. We believe in upholding the rich traditions of our heritage while embracing modernity, creating an inclusive environment for all who seek blessings and wish to serve.
